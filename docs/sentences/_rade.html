The sun was so hot it almost felt like it was a million degrees centigrade and the balustrade of the balcony had begun to abrade from all the heat.

My comrades and I performed a charade, pretending everything was alright when in truth we had been slowly degrading for months.

The masquerade parade had all the highest grades of elegance and glamour as the ladies and gentlemen strutted down the streets in their finery.

After his tirade, my father refused to trade any pleasantries and instead marched away in a retrograde direction.
